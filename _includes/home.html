Our Speakers

We’re delighted to announce our first confirmed speakers, more will be coming soon.

Kojo Idrissa

“Mastering Django by solving local problems”

Kojo (USA) is our first keynote speaker. He will be speaking about practices and patterns in problem-solving and how they apply across the globe.

Flavio Percoco and Honza Kral

“From idea to production”

Flavio (Italy) and Honza (Czech Republic) will speak about what’s needed to turn a software idea to a reality.

Anna Makarudze

“Navigating the open-source world as a minority”

Anna (Zimbabwe) spent several years working as the President of the Django Software Foundation. She will share her insights on how those in ethnic, gender and other minorities can succeed, and communities can support them.

Dawn Wages

“Supercharging your Python development environment with VS Code and Dev Containers”

Dawn (USA) will discuss developer tools for Python/Django programmers, and the benefits that Visual Studio Code’s Dev Containers can offer.

What’s happening at DjangoCon Africa

Talks

Three days of talks, on programming, technology, careers, society and business.

Workshops and tutorials

Hands-on training and learning sessions, led by experts in the field.

Collaboration

Work together on code, and contribute to major projects - including Django itself.

Django Girls

A training workshop aimed at women, providing an introduction to web application development. Tens of thousands of women across the world have had their introduction to Django through this hugely successful programme.

Discovery

DjangoCon Africa is an opportunity to discover places and people as well as technology. It will bring together participants from across Africa and the rest of the world, for mutual understanding of contexts, challenges and opportunities.
